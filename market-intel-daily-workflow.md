# Daily Workflow — Signal Forge

## Project name
Signal Forge (market-intel research OS)

## Purpose
Define how the team operates day-to-day to keep the research system productive, consistent, and sustainable.

---

## Recommended daily rhythm

### Morning scan
- scan headlines for items worth intake
- aim for 3–6 items per day maximum
- do not try to process everything
- prefer quality over quantity

### Intake per item
For each selected headline:
1. capture headline
2. run automation stub
3. normalize event
4. classify taxonomy
5. assign source tier

### Scout and Atlas pass
- scout normalizes 1–2 sentences
- atlas maps assets

### Pulse pass
- quick impact and confidence assessment
- decide fast-lane vs slow-lane

### End-of-day summary
- 1 paragraph per processed item
- final decision ladder result
- if slow-lane, attach study and evidence stubs

---

## Volume guidance
- minimum productive day: 2 cases processed
- standard day: 3–4 cases processed
- heavy day: 5–6 cases
- avoid processing more than 8 in one session unless running automation

---

## Fast-lane vs slow-lane balance
- fast-lane: about 60–70% of items
- slow-lane: about 30–40% of items

This is healthy because most headlines do not deserve deep treatment.

---

## Weekly rhythm
- 1 day per week: review case library
- 1 day per week: update taxonomy if new event types appeared
- 1 day per week: process evidence for existing studies
- 1 day per week: write a short research summary or lesson

---

## Monthly rhythm
- review decision ladder results
- archive stale cases
- upgrade study candidates toward simulation
- review false-positive patterns
- update source quality assessment

---

## Role rhythm in daily workflow
### Scout + Atlas
- process most items
- focus on fast coverage

### Pulse
- decide which items deserve deeper work

### Sage + Shield
- only work on slow-lane items
- quality over speed

### Main
- summarize daily output for the human
- ensure naming and case library stay consistent

---

## Human operator role
### Daily
- choose headlines
- approve intake
- decide which items get deeper treatment
- review main summary

### Weekly
- review false positives
- update source priorities
- approve case library updates

---

## Design rule
Automate file creation.
Automate naming.
Do not automate final judgment until the system has enough evidence.
