# Taxonomy Playbook Notes

## How to use taxonomy
When a new case is added:
1. assign `event_type`
2. assign `event_family`
3. identify the first-order impact path
4. list likely second-order paths
5. choose a tentative persistence profile
6. define the best validation method
7. note the most likely failure mode

## Why this matters
This structure helps the team avoid treating every headline the same way.
A supply shock, a policy hold, and a regulatory easing story can all look "bullish" somewhere, but they behave differently in:
- timing
- persistence
- cross-asset spread
- failure risk

## Practical use
- Scout helps identify the event family
- Atlas helps identify direct and indirect paths
- Pulse helps assign persistence and likely first-order market effect
- Sage uses the taxonomy to choose a research style
- Shield uses the taxonomy to challenge false analogies

## Warning
Do not force precision too early.
Taxonomy should improve comparison, not create fake certainty.
